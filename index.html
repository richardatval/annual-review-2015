Voluntary Action LeicesterShireAnnual Review2014-2015

Welcome

2014-15 has been another busy year for VAL. Here’s what we’ve been up to.

Creating a Thriving Local Sector

We work to support local VCS organisations of all shapes and sizes.

We supported 1,120 groups , generated £6,278,769 of new income and trained 708 people.

The Chinese Elderly Group

One of the groups we supported won £2,865 so that its social club could find a permanent home.

Learn more

Sending a Message

With our support, Musical Youth won £3,727. The funds allowed 20 children to speak out on online safety.

Learn more

Connecting the VCS

At VAL, we develop opportunities for the VCS to speak out, collaborate and learn.

474 people networked at 23 forums and 98 people joined us at Future Focus 2014.

"Well organised, well ordered and in an impressive venue that said to the VCS ‘we have a right to be in these kind of places"

- Future Focus Delegate

Supporting Communities

We’re helping local people shape the communities where they live.

£9,427 in grants awarded to empower St Matthews residents 21 communities supported to take over libraries 161 community members trained.

Football and Life Skills

St Matthews group Iqra Community Solutions won £2,328 to deliver a football and life skills project to young people in St Matthews.

Community Libraries

The assistance we have received from VAL [when setting up our library] has been first rate.

- Steve Mitchel, Rothley Library

Empowering Volunteers

We run a range of services to help local people make positive change happen by volunteering.

10,418 volunteering opportunities advertised, £4,590,768 contributed to the local economy by people we helped into volunteering 3,921 supported to find volunteering roles.

Independence for Jon

We helped 30 people like Jon find volunteering roles and become more independent through our work at the Me and My Learning Centre.

Learn more

King Richard III Reinterment

We recruited 70 volunteers who gave 695 hours to support the historic events surrounding King Richard III’s Reinterment.

I have had the most wonderful time and feel honoured to have been able to help even in such a small way.

- Volunteer for King Richard III Reinterment

Supporting Children, Young People & Families

Our Children, Young People and Families Team provides a range of services to help individuals, families and VCS groups succeed.

We had 47% increase in referrals New Service launched offering support around Education Health and Care Plan process. 2,000 people who support children and families trained.

Local Offer Live

Local Offer Leicester welcomed 700 people and 95 service providers across health, care, education and the VCS at their first annual event.

Learn more

Healthwatch

Healthwatch is the independent consumer champion created to gather and represent the views of the public.

We engaged 5,621 individuals at 200 activities and events.

Patient Views on LRI

Healthwatch Leicestershire staff and volunteers spent a week in Leicester Royal Infirmary (LRI) to hear the views of 262 patients.
